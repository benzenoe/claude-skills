# Edmund Bogen Mastermind Creator - Combined Skill

This combined skill merges the Edmund Bogen luxury real estate brand guidelines with the mastermind event creation system. Use this skill whenever creating mastermind invitations, workbooks, or any coaching content that needs to maintain brand consistency.

## PART 1: EDMUND BOGEN BRAND GUIDELINES

### Brand Overview

**Business**: The Edmund Bogen Team at Douglas Elliman Real Estate + Edmund's Mastermind & Group Coaching
**Location**: South Florida (Palm Beach to Miami)
**Markets**: 
- Luxury residential real estate
- Real estate coaching & mastermind programs
**Brand Position**: Professional luxury + approachable expertise + South Florida lifestyle

### Color Palette

#### Primary Colors (Use These First)

**Navy/Dark Blue** - `#1a3e5c`
- PRIMARY brand color for both real estate and coaching materials
- Use for: Headers, backgrounds, text overlays, professional sections
- Represents: Trust, stability, luxury, sophistication
- Usage: 60% of design elements

**Bright Blue/Cyan** - `#00a8e1`
- ACCENT color
- Use for: CTAs, vertical bars, highlights, links, interactive elements
- Represents: Modern, energetic, approachable
- Usage: 10-15% for emphasis

**White** - `#ffffff`
- NEUTRAL
- Use for: Text on dark backgrounds, clean sections, breathing room
- Usage: 25-30% for balance

#### Secondary Colors

**Black** - `#000000`
- Use for: Text on white backgrounds, dramatic headers
- Creates high-contrast, sophisticated look

**Light Gray** - `#f4f4f4` to `#e8e8e8`
- Use for: Background sections, subtle separators

#### Color Rules
1. ALWAYS maintain 60% navy, 30% white, 10% bright blue ratio
2. NEVER use warm colors (red, orange, yellow) except in property photos
3. Ensure WCAG AA contrast (4.5:1 minimum) for all text
4. Use navy with 40-60% opacity for photo overlays
5. CTAs must be bright blue (#00a8e1) with white text

### Typography

#### Font Family
**Primary**: Modern geometric sans-serif (Montserrat, Lato, or Gotham style)
- NEVER use serif fonts
- NEVER use script or decorative fonts
- Keep it clean, modern, and luxury-appropriate

#### Text Formatting Rules

**Headers & Display Text**
- Weight: Light (300) to Regular (400)
- Case: ALL CAPS for brand names and major headers
- Letter-spacing: Wide tracking (0.05-0.15em) for all-caps
- Size: 36-72px for hero headers
- Color: White on dark, Black on light
- Examples: "THE EDMUND BOGEN TEAM", "FEATURED LISTINGS", "EDMUND'S MASTERMIND"

**Body Text**
- Weight: Regular (400)
- Size: 16-18px minimum
- Line-height: 1.6-1.8
- Color: #333333 on white, #ffffff on dark
- Case: Normal sentence case

**Subheadings**
- Weight: Medium (500) to Semi-Bold (600)
- Size: 20-28px
- Letter-spacing: Slight (0.02-0.05em)
- Case: Title Case or Sentence case (NOT all caps)

**Buttons & CTAs**
- ALL CAPS
- Medium weight
- Wide letter-spacing
- Examples: "LEARN MORE", "JOIN MASTERMIND", "REGISTER NOW"

#### Typography Don'ts
- NEVER crowd text (minimum 16px body size)
- NEVER use more than 3 font sizes per section
- NEVER use all-caps for paragraphs or long text
- NEVER mix serif and sans-serif fonts

### Design Elements & Layout

#### Spacing
- Section padding: 80-120px vertical (desktop), 40-60px (mobile)
- Use 16px as base spacing unit (multiples of 8 or 16)
- Generous white space = luxury aesthetic
- NEVER crowd elements

#### Key Design Elements

**Vertical Accent Bars**
- 3-5px wide bright blue (#00a8e1) bars
- Placement: Left side of content sections
- Used for emphasis and visual interest

**Image Overlays**
- Navy/black gradient at 40-60% opacity
- Always ensures text readability
- Placement: Full image or bottom portion

**Buttons**
- Primary: Bright blue (#00a8e1) background, white text
- Secondary: Transparent with bright blue border
- Padding: 16px vertical, 40px horizontal
- Border-radius: 0-4px (minimal or none)
- Hover: Darken 10-15%

### Writing Style & Voice

#### Brand Voice
- **Professional**: Knowledgeable, experienced, trustworthy
- **Approachable**: Friendly, helpful, not pretentious
- **Confident**: Proven track record, market expertise
- **Service-oriented**: Client-focused, attentive

#### Messaging Principles
1. Emphasize results: "Proven track record", "Get the most for your property"
2. Geographic authority: "Throughout South Florida", "From Palm Beach to Miami"
3. Personal touch: "The Edmund Bogen Team" / "Edmund's Mastermind" (not impersonal)
4. Partnership language: "We can help you" (collaborative)

#### Writing Guidelines
- **Headlines**: Direct, benefit-focused, action-oriented
- **Body**: Clear, informative, no jargon
- **CTAs**: Strong verbs, clear next steps
- **Tone**: Professional but warm, confident but approachable
- NEVER: Hyperbole (except in mastermind invitations), overpromising, complex terminology

## PART 2: MASTERMIND EVENT CREATOR

### What This Creates

1. **Email Invitations** - Aggressive, competitive-threat positioning that sells transformation and revenue outcomes
2. **Implementation Workbooks** - 15-20 page PDF-ready coursebooks with teaching content, prompts, and tracking systems

### Required Information Gathering

Before creating materials, gather these details:

#### Event Basics
- **Topic/Title**: What is the mastermind session about?
- **Date & Time**: When is it scheduled? (Include timezone)
- **Zoom Link**: Meeting URL and credentials
- **Workbook Link**: Where will the workbook be hosted?

#### Content Details
- **Main Problem**: What pain point does this solve?
- **Key Tools/Systems**: What will attendees learn? (3-5 specific tools, frameworks, or strategies)
- **Transformation Promise**: What outcome will they achieve?
- **Competitive Angle**: Who is already using this while others fall behind?

#### Supporting Elements
- **Statistics/Data**: Any compelling numbers to include?
- **Target Audience**: Realtors? Broader entrepreneurs? Specific segment?
- **Implementation Timeline**: 30 days? 90 days? Immediate?

### Workbook Brand Guidelines

**Visual Design:**
- Colors: Navy (#1a3e5c) for headers, bright blue (#00a8e1) for accents, white backgrounds
- Typography: Sans-serif, all-caps for major headers with wide letter-spacing
- Maintain generous white space
- Use checkboxes, fill-in lines, and structured note-taking sections

**Content Tone:**
- First 2-3 pages of each section = TEACHING (psychology, research, why it works)
- Then practical exercises and prompts
- Revenue-focused language throughout ("Your Bank Account", "Profit-Generating")
- Quantify everything (time savings, dollar amounts, percentages)
- Include ROI calculators and value tracking

**PROMPTS Requirements:**
- MUST include 4-6 copy-paste prompts per tool/system
- Format in code blocks for easy copying
- Label each prompt by use case
- Make them specific and immediately usable
- This is what attendees love most - don't skimp

**Worksheet Elements:**
- Lots of fill-in-the-blank sections
- Lines for writing (_____________________)
- Checkboxes for tracking
- Space for calculations ($______)
- Commitment sections with signature lines

### PDF Formatting for Print

- Standard 8.5" x 11" letter size
- 1" margins all sides
- Headers with Edmund's Mastermind branding
- Page numbers in footer
- Section breaks clearly marked
- Print-friendly (no full-page dark backgrounds)
- Optimize for black & white printing with good contrast

### Page Count Management

To keep workbooks at 15-20 pages:
- If 3 tools: 3-5 pages each = 9-15 pages for content + 5 pages for intro/implementation = 14-20 total
- If 4 tools: 3 pages each = 12 pages for content + 5 pages for intro/implementation = 17 total
- If 5 tools: 2-3 pages each = 10-15 pages for content + 5 pages for intro/implementation = 15-20 total

Adjust depth per tool based on complexity, but maintain the teaching ‚Üí prompts ‚Üí worksheets structure for each.
